| Risk exposure | 风险敞口 | The amount of potential loss from a specific risk |
| Mitigation | 风险缓释 | Actions taken to reduce the impact or likelihood of a risk |
| Residual risk | 剩余风险 | Risk remaining after controls are applied |
| Key Risk Indicator (KRI) | 关键风险指标 | Metrics used to monitor risk levels |
| Segregation of duties | 职责分离 | Splitting critical functions among different people to prevent fraud |

| Materiality | 重要性水平 | The threshold above which missing/incorrect information could influence decisions |
| Control environment | 控制环境 | The overall attitude and awareness of an organization toward internal controls |

## 四、Interview Q&A 面试问答

### Q1: Why do you want to work in Web3/crypto?

> **EN:** I believe we're witnessing a fundamental shift in how financial systems operate. Web3 introduces concepts — self-custody, permissionless access, programmable money — that traditional finance simply cannot offer. What excites me most is the intersection of risk management and blockchain. The security challenges in Web3 are unprecedented: smart contract vulnerabilities, oracle manipulation, bridge attacks. Solving these requires a hybrid mindset — understanding both traditional risk frameworks and the unique properties of decentralized systems. That's exactly where my background fits.

> **ZH:** 我认为我们正在见证金融体系运作方式的根本转变。Web3 引入了传统金融无法提供的概念——自主托管、无需许可的访问、可编程货币。最让我兴奋的是风险管理和区块链的交叉点。Web3 中的安全挑战前所未有：智能合约漏洞、预言机操纵、跨链桥攻击。解决这些问题需要混合思维——既理解传统风险框架，又理解去中心化系统的独特属性。这正是我的背景所适合的。

### Q2: What's the biggest difference between traditional and crypto risk control?

> **EN:** There are three fundamental differences:
>
> 1. **Irreversibility**: In traditional finance, transactions can be reversed or frozen. In crypto, once a transaction is confirmed on-chain, it's final. This means prevention is everything — you can't undo a hack.
>
> 2. **Pseudonymity**: Traditional finance has KYC as the foundation. In crypto, you're dealing with addresses, not identities. Risk assessment must be behavior-based, not identity-based.
>
> 3. **Attack surface**: Traditional risk focuses on market, credit, and operational risk. Crypto adds smart contract risk, oracle risk, governance risk, and protocol-level economic attacks.
>
> However, the core principles are the same: identify risks, measure exposure, implement controls, monitor continuously, and respond quickly.

> **ZH:** 有三个根本区别：
> 1. 不可逆性：传统金融中交易可以撤销或冻结。加密世界中链上确认即最终——不能撤回黑客攻击。
> 2. 匿名性：传统金融以 KYC 为基础。加密世界中面对的是地址而非身份。风险评估必须基于行为而非身份。
> 3. 攻击面：传统风控关注市场、信用和操作风险。加密世界还增加了智能合约风险、预言机风险、治理风险和协议层面的经济攻击。
>
> 但核心原则相同：识别风险、衡量敞口、实施控制、持续监控、快速响应。

### Q3: Describe a challenging risk situation you handled.

> **EN:** At Tsingshan, I was involved in reviewing the company's hedging positions during a period of extreme nickel price volatility. The situation was similar to what became the 2022 LME nickel crisis — margin calls were escalating rapidly, and we had to assess whether our physical delivery capacity could cover our short futures positions.

This analysis was escalated to senior management, who then negotiated with banks for additional credit lines and with the exchange for position restructuring. The lesson I took from this is that in risk management, speed of analysis is critical — you need to quantify exposure before market conditions force your hand.

> **ZH:** 在青山控股，我参与了镍价极端波动期间公司套保头寸的审查。情况类似于后来发生的 2022 年 LME 镍危机——追加保证金要求急剧上升，我们必须评估实物交割能力能否覆盖空头期货头寸。
>
> 我主导了分析，将每份期货合约映射到具体的实物库存和产能，识别出约 15% 的潜在交割缺口。这一分析被上报给高级管理层，随后与银行谈判争取额外授信额度，并与交易所协商头寸重组。我的收获是：风控中分析速度至关重要——你必须在市场条件逼迫你行动之前量化风险敞口。

### Q4: How do you detect market manipulation in crypto?

> **EN:** Market manipulation detection in crypto requires a multi-layered approach:
>
> 1. **On-chain analysis**: Look at transaction graphs. Wash trading often shows the same funds circulating between connected addresses. I use tools like Nansen, Dune Analytics, and custom scripts to trace fund flows.
>
> 2. **Order book analysis**: Monitor order-to-trade ratios. Spoofing shows high order placement with low execution rates (>95% cancellation rate is a red flag).
>
> 3. **Cross-exchange arbitrage monitoring**: If the same address is consistently profiting from price discrepancies across exchanges, it may indicate coordinated manipulation.
>
> 4. **Behavioral clustering**: Group addresses by device fingerprint, IP patterns, and deposit/withdrawal behaviors. Manipulation rings often share infrastructure.
>
> 5. **Statistical anomaly detection**: Use Z-score and IQR methods to flag unusual trading volumes, price movements, and order patterns.
>
> The key is combining all these signals — no single indicator is sufficient.

> **ZH:** 加密市场操纵检测需要多层方法：
> 1. 链上分析：查看交易图谱，对敲通常显示相同资金在关联地址间循环。
> 2. 订单簿分析：监控挂单成交比，幌骗显示高挂单低成交率（>95% 撤单率是红色信号）。
> 3. 跨交易所套利监控：同一地址持续从价差获利可能表明协同操纵。
> 4. 行为聚类：通过设备指纹、IP 模式、充提行为分组。
> 5. 统计异常检测：使用 Z-score 和 IQR 标记异常交易量、价格变动和订单模式。

### Q5: What is your approach to smart contract risk assessment?

> **EN:** I follow a structured framework:
>
> **Pre-deployment:**
> - Code audit (manual + automated tools like Slither, Mythril)
> - Check for known vulnerability patterns: reentrancy, integer overflow, access control, flash loan vectors
> - Verify oracle data sources (TWAP vs spot price)
> - Review governance parameters (timelock duration, proposal threshold, multi-sig setup)
> - Test with forked mainnet environment
>
> **Post-deployment:**
> - Set up real-time monitoring via Forta, Tenderly, or OpenZeppelin Defender
> - Monitor for unusual function calls, large transfers, owner operations
> - Track TVL changes and token holder concentration
> - Set up on-chain alerts for governance proposals and parameter changes
>
> **Ongoing:**
> - Regular re-audits after upgrades
> - Monitor for new attack vectors in the ecosystem
> - Maintain an incident response playbook with clear escalation paths

> **ZH:** 我遵循结构化框架：
> 部署前：代码审计 + 已知漏洞模式检查 + 预言机来源验证 + 治理参数审查 + 分叉主网测试
> 部署后：实时监控（Forta/Tenderly）+ 异常调用/大额转账/owner 操作 + TVL 变化和持币集中度
> 持续：升级后重新审计 + 生态新攻击向量监控 + 事件响应预案

### Q6: How do you handle a situation where business goals conflict with risk controls?

> **EN:** This is a common tension. My approach is:
>
> 1. **Quantify the risk**: Instead of saying "this is risky," I present specific numbers — potential loss amount, probability, historical precedents.
> 2. **Propose alternatives**: Don't just say no. Suggest a modified approach that achieves the business goal with acceptable risk (e.g., phased rollout, lower limits, additional monitoring).
> 3. **Escalate with options**: Present to leadership as "Option A = faster launch with X risk, Option B = slower launch with Y protection."
> 4. **Document everything**: Ensure the decision, rationale, and risk acceptance are recorded for audit trail.
>
> Risk management is not about blocking business — it's about enabling business safely.

> **ZH:** 这是常见矛盾。我的方法：
> 1. 量化风险：不说"这有风险"，而说"潜在损失 X，概率 Y，历史先例 Z"
> 2. 提出替代方案：不说不行，而是"可以这样做，如果加上这些控制"
> 3. 升级汇报带选项：方案 A = 快速上线 + X 风险，方案 B = 慢速上线 + Y 保护
> 4. 全程留痕：决策、理由、风险接受都记录可追溯

### 5.2 描述一个成功项目 / Describe a Successful Project

> **EN:** One project I'm proud of was building an automated alert triage system for our exchange's risk monitoring. Before this, our analysts were spending 70% of their time reviewing false positive alerts.
>
> I led the effort to:
> 1. Analyze 3 months of historical alert data to identify patterns in false positives
> 2. Implement a scoring model that combined multiple risk signals into a single severity score
> 3. Create automated workflows: low-score alerts were auto-resolved with notes, medium-score went to L1 analysts, high-score triggered immediate escalation
>
> **Result**: False positive review time dropped by 60%, and our mean time to respond to genuine threats improved from 15 minutes to under 3 minutes. The analysts could finally focus on actual risks instead of noise.

> **ZH:** 我引以为豪的一个项目是为交易所风控搭建自动化告警分类系统。此前，分析员 70% 的时间花在审查误报上。
>
> 我主导了：1) 分析 3 个月历史告警数据识别误报模式；2) 实施评分模型将多个风险信号合并为单一严重性评分；3) 创建自动化流程——低分自动解决+备注，中分→L1 分析员，高分→立即升级。
>
> 结果：误报审查时间下降 60%，真实威胁平均响应时间从 15 分钟降至 3 分钟以内。

### 5.3 描述处理危机 / Describe Handling a Crisis

> **EN:** During a market volatility event, we noticed a cluster of accounts rapidly withdrawing large amounts of USDT — all within a 10-minute window. The pattern suggested a coordinated exit, potentially linked to a security breach or insider information.
>
> I immediately:
> 1. Escalated to the head of risk and CTO (within 2 minutes of detection)
> 2. Initiated a temporary withdrawal freeze for the flagged accounts
> 3. Traced the fund flows on-chain to identify the source of funds
> 4. Coordinated with the compliance team to file STRs
> 5. Briefed customer support on how to handle inquiries from affected users

## 七、Case Studies 案例描述

### 7.1 伦镍事件 / LME Nickel Crisis

> **EN:** In March 2022, nickel prices on the London Metal Exchange surged from around $30,000 to over $100,000 per ton in just two days. This was triggered by a massive short squeeze — a major Chinese producer, Tsingshan Holding Group, had built a large short position to hedge its physical nickel production.
>
> When the price spiked, Tsingshan faced margin calls estimated at $8 billion. The LME took the unprecedented step of suspending trading and canceling billions in trades.
>
> **Risk Control Lessons:**
> 1. Position concentration risk — the short position was too large relative to market liquidity
> 2. Delivery mismatch — Tsingshan's physical nickel was not LME-deliverable grade
> 3. Margin adequacy — stress scenarios were not extreme enough
> 4. Counterparty transparency — OTC positions were not visible to the exchange
>
> This case fundamentally changed how commodity hedgers think about tail risk.

> **ZH:** 2022 年 3 月，LME 镍价两日内从约 3 万美元/吨飙升至超 10 万美元/吨，由大规模逼空引发——中国大型生产商青山控股建立了大量空头头寸来对冲实物镍生产。价格暴涨后，青山面临约 80 亿美元的追保要求。LME 采取前所未有的措施暂停交易并取消数十亿美元交易。
>
> 风控教训：1) 头寸集中度过高；2) 交割品不匹配（非 LME 可交割级）；3) 保证金压力情景不够极端；4) 场外头寸不透明。此案从根本上改变了大宗商品套保者对尾部风险的认识。

### 7.2 FTX 崩盘 / FTX Collapse

> **EN:** FTX, once the third-largest crypto exchange, collapsed in November 2022 after revelations that customer funds had been commingled with its sister trading firm Alameda Research. Key failures included:
>
> - No segregation of customer and corporate funds
> - Related-party transactions without proper disclosure
> - Falsified financial statements
> - No independent board or risk committee
>
> **Risk Control Lessons:**
> - Segregation of duties and funds is non-negotiable
> - Related-party transactions require enhanced scrutiny
> - Proof of reserves alone is insufficient without proof of liabilities
> - Offshore registration does not exempt from basic governance standards

> **ZH:** FTX 崩溃的核心教训：客户资金与公司资金混用、关联交易未披露、财务报表造假、无独立董事会或风控委员会。风控启示：职责和资金分离不可妥协、关联交易需加强审查、仅有储备证明不够还需要负债证明、离岸注册不免除基本治理标准。

### 7.3 Wormhole 跨链桥攻击

> **EN:** In February 2022, the Wormhole bridge was exploited for $326 million — one of the largest DeFi hacks. The attacker exploited a signature verification vulnerability to mint wrapped ETH on Solana without depositing the equivalent on Ethereum.
>
> **Risk Control Lessons:**
> - Bridge code requires the highest level of audit scrutiny
> - Multi-signature validation with independent parties
> - Real-time monitoring of mint/burn ratios across chains
> - Circuit breakers for anomalous bridge activity
> - Bug bounty programs to incentivize responsible disclosure

> **ZH:** 2022 年 2 月 Wormhole 跨链桥被攻击损失 3.26 亿美元。攻击者利用签名验证漏洞在 Solana 上铸造包装 ETH 而无需在以太坊上存入等值资产。风控教训：桥代码需最高级别审计、多方独立多签验证、实时监控跨链铸造/销毁比率、异常活动熔断机制、漏洞赏金激励负责任披露。

### 8.1 面试对话 / Interview Dialogue

> **Interviewer:** Tell me about your risk control experience.
>
> **You:** I have about three years of experience split between traditional industry and Web3. At Tsingshan, I focused on supply chain auditing and commodity hedging risk — very quantitative, very process-driven. In Web3, I've worked on CEX risk monitoring systems, covering market manipulation detection and KYC/AML compliance. The combination has given me a unique perspective: I understand both the rigorous control frameworks of traditional finance and the fast-moving, technically complex nature of crypto risk.

> **Interviewer:** What's your approach to a new risk that you haven't seen before?
>
> **You:** First, I assess the blast radius — what's the worst that could happen, and how quickly? Second, I look for similar incidents in the industry — almost nothing is truly unprecedented. Third, I implement a containment measure immediately, even if it's imperfect, to buy time for a proper solution. Finally, I document everything so the next person doesn't start from zero. Speed of response often matters more than perfection of response.

> **Interviewer:** Where do you see yourself in three years?
>
> **You:** I want to become a go-to expert in Web3 risk management — someone who can bridge the gap between traditional risk frameworks and crypto-native security challenges. I see the industry evolving toward institutional-grade risk controls, and I want to be part of building that infrastructure. Whether that's at an exchange, a DeFi protocol, or a risk analytics firm, I'm excited about the chance to shape how this industry matures.
